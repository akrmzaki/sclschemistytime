HYDROGEN


*Discovered by: Henry Cavendish in 1766*

PHYSICAL PROPERTIES
1)The hydrogen gas consists of a proton and an electron.
2)Colourless
3)Has no smell or taste
4)Atomic mass= 1.00784 u
5)Atomic size (radius)= 53pm
6) Nonmetallic element
7)Bad conductor of electricity
8)Poor conductor of heat
9)Smallest element in the modern periodic table
10)Has the lowest density in all elements 0.08375 kg/m³at 20°C
11)Melting point=- 259.2 °C (low)
12)Can exist in liquid state
13)Boiling point =- 252.8 °C (low)
14)insoluble in water
15)lighter than air
16)Highly flammable gas but not a supporter of combustion
17)Nonpoisonous gas
18)Most abundant element
19)The gas consists of 75% of orthohydrogen and the rest of the 25 percent of parahydrogen at the standard temperature and pressure.
20)The gas is present in the atmosphere in the volume of one part per million.


Chemical PROPERTIES
Electronegativity (according to Pauling)=2.1
Isotopes = 3
Atomic no. =1
Diatomic gas
Bond with metalloids and metals forming hydrides
Hydrogen extracts its electron and emits the H+ ion upon oxidation.
It can explode when exposed to sunlight in the mixture of chlorines and hydrogen gas
500 °C is the combustion occurring temperature of the H2 gas also known as the auto ignition temperature of the hydrogen.
Ultraviolet rays that are invisible to the naked eye are emitted by the pure hydrogen-oxygen flames.
Forms compounds with most of the elements despite its stability
Lose an electron to form a proton
Gain an electron to form a hydride ion or form a covalent bond or polar covalent electron-pair bond.


Uses of hydrogen
Hydrogen is significantly used in the synthesis of ammonia.
To extract fat, a huge amount of H2 is consumed during the catalytic hydrogenation.
As rocket propellant by nuclear form energy, the hydrogen also gets consumed as rocket fuel when it is combined with oxygen.
In internal combustion engines, the hydrogen is burnt in the form of fuel.
Further used in the production of nitric acid and nitrogenous fertilizers.
H2 is used in the preparation of a useful chemical, hydrogen chloride.


Reactions of Hydrogen


Reaction of hydrogen with oxygen
2H2(g) + O2(g) ---> 2H2O(l)
Reaction of Hydrogen with water , bases and acids
Hydrogen doesn't react with water , bases or acids in ordinary conditions
Reaction of Hydrogen with Halogens
H2(g) + F2(g) ---> 2HF(g)
H2 +Br2--->2HBr
H2(g)+2ICl(g)--->2HCl(g)+I2(g)
