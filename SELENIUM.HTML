SELENIUM


PROPERTIES
Selenium is a soft metalloid, found in several allotropic forms. Selenium is essential in the diet in small amounts, but at levels of more than 5 mg, it can be toxic. High levels of selenium in the body can produce foul breath and body odor, as a result of the production of volatile dimethylselenide, 2Se. In areas where the soil is particularly rich in selenium , there are some plants that are particularly efficient at pulling selenium from the soil. When cattle eat these plants, the develop some of the symptoms of selenium poisoning, including erratic behavior, aggression, nervousness, lethargy, and loss of balance .
