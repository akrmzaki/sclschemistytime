POTASSIUM


*discovered by: Sir Davy in 1808*
The main mining area used to be Germany, now most of it comes from Canada, USA, and Chile.

Physical properties
1)An alkali metal
2)Atomic mass: 39.0983 u
3)Atomic size: 280 pm
4)Density: 860 kg/m3 at 0 °C (second lowest density of elements)
5)Melting point: 63.2 °C (low)
6)Boiling point: 760 °C (low)
7)Soft and shiny element
8)Silvery/White metal
9)Monovalent
10)Occurs in the Earth's crust as minerals, such as feldspars and clays
11)Most of this element found in the sea (0.75 g/liter).
12)Fine conductor of heat and electricity
13)Potassium imparts a lavender colour to a flame, and its vapour is green.
14)Malleable& ductile metal

CHEMICAL PROPERTIES
1)Atomic no.: 19
2)Isotopes: 5
3)Electronegativity (according to Pauling ): 0.8
4)Monoatomic element
5)It is one of the most reactive and electropositive of metals.
6)Reacts instantly with water releasing hydrogen.
7)Soluble in water
8)Never found free in nature, but is obtained by electrolysis of the chloride or hydroxide.
9)Kept under oil or geese
10)Highly Flammable and a fire hazard
11)Dissolves in water forming alkaline solution

Uses of Potassium
Most of potassium (95 %) goes into fertilizers. Potassium carbonate goes into glass manufacture, especially the glass used to make televisions, while potassium hydroxide is used to make liquid soaps and detergents. Other potassium salts are used in baking, photography and tanning leather, and to make iodize salts. In all cases it is the negative anion, not the potassium, which is the key to their use.
