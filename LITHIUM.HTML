LITHIUM


*discovered by: Johan August Arfvedson in 1817 *
Many of its properties are similar to that of the alkaline earth metals than those of its group.

Physical properties
1)From the alkali metals
2)Atomic mass=6.941 u
3) Atomic size (radius)= 182pm
4)Monovalent element
5)Exists in solid state at 20 °C
6)Density= 530 kg m?3 at 20 °C (Lowest among all alkali metals)
7)Melting point =180.5 °C (lowest melting temperature among metals)
8)Boiling point =1342 °C ( highest of all alkali metals)
9)Lightest solid metal
10)Soft, silvery, white
11)Doesn’t occur as the metal in nature, but is found combined in small amounts in igneous rocks.
12)Good conductor of heat and electricity

Chemical properties
1)Chemically active element
2)Never found in its pure form
3)Isotopes=2
4)Electronegativity( according to Pauling)= 1.0
5)Atomic no. = 3
6)Only alkaline metal that reacts with nitrogen at ambient temperature to produce a black nitrure.
7)It reacts easily with hydrogen at almost 500ºC to form lithium hydride.
8)React with water is extremely vigorous.
9)Lithium reacts directly with the carbon to produce the carbure.
10)It binds easily with halogens and forms halogenures with light emission.
11)It also reacts with acetylenic compounds, forming lithium acetylures, which are important in vitamin A synthesis
12)Soluble in short chain aliphatic amines, like etilamine.
13)Insoluble in hydrocarbons
14)Can’t be kept under Kerosene as bec. of low density
15)Kept under Paraffin oil to avoid its reaction with moist air.

Uses of lithium
It's used to treat mood disorders such as: mania (feeling highly excited, overactive or distracted) hypo-mania (similar to mania, but less severe) regular periods of depression, where treatment with other medicines has not worked.
