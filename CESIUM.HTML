CESIUM

*discovered by: Fustov Kirchhoff in 1860*


Physical properties
1)From alkali metals
2)Atomic mass: 132.90545 u
3)Atomic size: 343 pm
4)Density: 1 930 kg/m³at 20°C (highest density)
5)Melting point: 28.5 °C (low)
6)Boiling point: 669 °C(low)
7)Valency : monovalent
8)Solid at STP
9)Silvery/ gold metal
10)Soft element
11)Doesn’t occur freely in nature
12)Malleable& ductile metal
13)Good conductor of heat & electricity
14)At slightly above room temperature, cesium exists in the liquid state.
15)Strongest metal in the periodic table
16)Highest vapor pressure


Chemical properties
1)Atomic no. 55
2)Isotopes:12
3)Electronegativity(according to Pauling): 0.7
(least electronegative element) 4)The most electropositive and most alkaline element.
5)Reacts explosively with cold water.
6)Reacts with ice at temperatures above -116°C
7)Cesium reacts with the halogens to form a fluoride, chloride, bromide, and iodide.
8)Cesium metal oxidized rapidly when exposed to the air
9)Kept under a layer of kerosene or mineral oil or in a vacuum
10)Cesium reacts explosively with water vapor in the air at temperatures above -116°C.
11)Monoatomic
12)Mildly poisonous and has a limit use
13)Turns its color into blue/violet when it burns

Uses of Cesium
1)used as a catalyst in the hydrogenation of a few organic compounds.
2)the metal can be used in ion propulsion systems. used in atomic clocks.
3)the metal is used as a "getter" in electron tubes because of its high oxygen affinity.
4)used in photoelectric cells and vacuum tubes.
5) used in IR lamps.

General information of cesium
Cesium is much less abundant than the other alkali metals but it is still more common than elements like arsenic, iodine and uranium. Few cesium mineral are known, pollucite is the main: they are silicate magmas cooled from granites. World production of cesium compounds is just 20 tons per year, coming mainly from the Bernice Lake (Canada) with a little from Zimbabwe and South-West Africa.

2Cs(s) + Br2(g) --->CsBr(s)
2Cs(s) + Cl2(g) --->CsCl(s)
2Cs(s) + I2(g) --->CsI(s)

Cs+HNO3--->CsNO3+H2
